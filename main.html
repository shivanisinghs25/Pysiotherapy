A Brief History of Physiotherapy


The earliest documented origins of Physiotherapy as a professional group, dates way back to Per Henrik Ling (“father of Swedish Gymnastics”) who founded the Royal Central Institute of Gymnastics in 1813, for Massage, Manipulation and Exercise. Sweden’s National Board of Health and Welfare recognized its potential and Physiotherapists were given official registration in 1887. Over the decades that follow, Physiotherapy has surged ahead and become the popular health care profession we all know and love today.



What is Physiotherapy?


Put simply, Physiotherapy is a healthcare profession that involves diagnoses and treatment of people who experience difficulty moving or performing normal activities due to a condition or illness – what makes it truly unique though, is that people are often restored back to health through Physiotherapy without the use of drugs or surgery. Internationally, Physiotherapy is sometimes known as ‘Physical Therapy’ and Physiotherapists as ‘Physical Therapists’.
